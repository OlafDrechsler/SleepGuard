Do you often fall asleep while watching videos or listening to music on your phone? SleepGuard makes sure your device doesn't keep running all night long.

At an interval you choose, a small red circle appears on top of whatever you are watching. Tap it and playback continues as normal. Don't tap it within a few seconds - because you have fallen asleep - and SleepGuard locks the screen, just like pressing the power button. Your video stops and your phone is protected for the rest of the night.

There is also an Audio mode for music, audiobooks and podcasts: instead of a button, SleepGuard works as a simple sleep timer that stops playback after your chosen interval — even with the screen off.

WHY YOU NEED IT

* YouTube and other apps with autoplay: Without SleepGuard, one video starts the next and the next and the next ... until morning. In the worst case the battery is drained empty and your alarm can no longer go off when you need it. SleepGuard stops the chain as soon as you stop responding.

* Netflix, Prime Video and other streaming apps: Fall asleep mid-episode and you wake up not knowing where you dozed off. You waste time scrubbing back to find the spot - sometimes sitting through long ads again and again. SleepGuard locks the screen right when you don't respond, so your streaming app knows where to continue.

HOW IT WORKS

1. Set how often the check button should appear (e.g. every 5 minutes).
2. Set how many seconds you have to react before the screen locks (e.g. 30 seconds).
3. Choose where on the screen the button appears.
4. Start SleepGuard and keep watching.

FEATURES

* Two modes: Video (wake-check button over any app) and Audio (sleep timer for music, audiobooks and podcasts, works also with the screen off)
* Adjustable interval and reaction time
* Eight button positions to choose from
* Reliable timing even in the background (uses the system alarm)
* 29 interface languages: all 24 official EU languages plus Russian, Japanese, Chinese (Simplified & Traditional) and Turkish
* Completely free, no ads, no tracking, no accounts

PRIVACY

SleepGuard has NO internet permission at all. It cannot send or receive any data. It does exactly two things: show a button and lock the screen. It collects nothing.

PERMISSIONS EXPLAINED

* Display over other apps: to show the red circle on top of your video.
* Device admin (screen lock only): to lock the screen like the power button.
* Foreground service & exact alarm: to make the check button appear on time.
* Ignore battery optimization (optional): so the button still works reliably while another app is in the foreground.
* Notifications (Android 13+): to show the ongoing "SleepGuard is active" notification.

THE IDEA / THE NAME

The idea for the app comes from a safety system used on trains. In the driver’s cab, there is a button that the driver must press at regular intervals, otherwise the train will automatically stop. By pressing the button, the driver confirms that he or she is awake. Colloquially, this button is called a “dead man’s switch,” and so the app is also called “SleepGuard – dead man’s switch.”
